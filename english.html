Non-Govermental Organization

The NGO ‘EcoInitiative’ is a voluntary public association the main goal of which is to support national, regional, and local environmental initiatives for sustainable development through research, education and awareness-raising activities among population of all ages, including those with special needs, and of different social status.

CURRENT ACTIVITIES
Enhancing Eco-Skills Through Cross-Border Collaboration between V4 and Ukraine
Project objective: participants from Poland, Slovakia, and Ukraine will collaborate to identify eco-problems in the regions, find ways to solve them, and improve the development of eco-skills through online events and a scientific summer school at the Pomeranian University in Słupsk. This project will promote cross-border exchange of best practices, and expand the opportunities of individuals and communities in implementing environmental practices.

Funding: carried out with financial support from International Visegrad Fund’s Visegrad+ (https://www.visegradfund.org/).

Partners for project implementation: Sumy State Pedagogical University named after A. S. Makarenko (Ukraine), University of St. Cyril and Methodius in Trnava (Slovakia), Pomeranian University in Słupsk (Poland).

Key stages of the project:
  1. International online webinar.
  2. International online forum.
  3. International scientific summer school at Pomeranian University in Słupsk (Poland).
  4. Preparation and publication of a collection of scientific materials based on the results of the International Scientific Summer School at Pomeranian University in Słupsk (Poland).
  5. International online symposium.
  6. Preparation and publication of an open-access electronic manual, which will include a wide range of topics related to environmental conservation, ecological awareness, and practical strategies for implementing environmental practices in everyday life in the form of research papers on thematic studies, practical recommendations, and more, provided by experts, researchers, educators, and project participants.

Recruitment of Participants for the International Summer Science School “Best Practices of the V4 Countries and Ukraine in Developing Eco-Skills”
As part of the international grant project “Enhancing Eco-Skills Through Cross-Border Collaboration between V4 and Ukraine” No. 22420189, implemented with the financial support of the International Visegrad Fund (beneficiary: NGO “EcoInitiative,” headed by Associate Professor Valentyna Toryanyk; project partners: A. S. Makarenko Sumy State Pedagogical University (Ukraine), University of St. Cyril and Methodius in Trnava (Slovakia), and Pomeranian University in Słupsk (Poland)), the recruitment of participants for the International Summer Science School “Best Practices of the V4 Countries and Ukraine in Developing Eco-Skills” is announced.

Location: Pomeranian University in Słupsk, Republic of Poland
Dates: 8–14 June 2025

Purpose and Objectives of the Summer School
The Summer Science School aims to promote cross-border exchange of experience in developing eco-skills among participants from Poland, Slovakia, and Ukraine. The program includes in-depth study of environmental concepts, practical skills in sustainable development, and innovative approaches to eco-education through the integration of theoretical knowledge and practical experience. The main objectives of the school are to exchange the best European and Ukrainian practices for achieving eco-sustainability, develop interdisciplinary cooperation among professionals from various fields, establish a network of international scientific collaboration in environmental education, and create scientific materials for further implementation in the educational process.

Invitation to participate is extended to

  1. University faculty members from partner institutions are invited to participate, including lecturers of natural sciences and related disciplines, as well as researchers engaged in sustainable development and environmental education. Participation of specialists with experience in implementing eco-oriented projects and initiatives is especially encouraged.
  2. Bachelor’s and master’s students are invited to participate. These include higher education seekers studying natural sciences and environmental specialties, as well as future lecturers of natural science disciplines. Priority is given to students with high academic achievements and an active stance on environmental sustainability issues.
Program
The Summer Science School program is designed as a comprehensive educational course combining expert lectures by leading scientists, interactive workshops, field research, and practical sessions. Participants will have the opportunity to visit environmentally significant sites in the Pomeranian Voivodeship, including the Słowiński National Park, the management of the Słupia River valley, and the wastewater treatment facilities in the city of Słupsk. A detailed event program with the schedule of lectures, workshops, and excursions is available via the provided link.
Program

Participation Conditions
Each partner university is invited to send 12 participants to the Summer Science School.
The Organizing Committee provides participants with:
  1. free accommodation in the dormitory of the Pomeranian University in Słupsk;
  2. full meals provided throughout the entire duration of the school; reimbursement of transportation expenses within the scope of the school program;
  3. educational and informational materials;
  4. certificates of successful completion of the school program.
Language of communication: English (basic proficiency required).

Selection Criteria and Application Procedure
  1. For academic and teaching staff, experience in the field of environmental education or related areas is required. Scientific publications on sustainable development and environmental education are considered an advantage. Applicants must also submit a motivation letter explaining their reasons for participation.
  2. For students, a high level of academic achievement and active participation in research on sustainable development issues or involvement in environmental initiatives are expected. A motivation letter is also required.

Deadlines and Application Submission Procedure
  1. Applications are accepted until May 15, 2025, inclusive.
  2. Selection results will be announced by May 20, 2025.
  3. Confirmation of participation is due by May 25, 2025.
  4. Departure to the venue is scheduled for June 7, 2025.
  5. The study period is from June 8 to June 14, 2025.
  6. Return is scheduled for June 15, 2025.
Documents for participation should be submitted to the project coordinator's email: olena.babenko@sspu.edu.ua

Expected Outcomes
The participants of the summer scientific school will contribute to the creation of an international scientific and educational space, promote the development of academic mobility, and help shape joint approaches to addressing modern environmental challenges. As a result of the school’s work, it is planned to prepare a collection of scientific materials and create an open online resource for teachers, researchers, and students, with the aim of building long-term partnerships between the participating universities on issues of ecological sustainability.

The organizing committee reserves the right to make changes to the program of the International Summer Scientific School “Best Practices of the V4 Countries and Ukraine in Developing Eco-Skills”and the terms of participation, with mandatory notification of the participants.

EcoInitiative NGO Members Participate in the Second Event of the International Grant Project "Enhancing Eco-Skills Through Cross-Border Cooperation between V4 and Ukraine" No. 22420189
On March 26, 2025, the second event of the International Grant Project "Enhancing Eco-Skills Through Cross-Border Cooperation between V4 and Ukraine" No. 22420189 took place - an international online forum titled "PATHWAYS TO SUSTAINABILITY: REGIONAL INSIGHTS AND BEST PRACTICE."

The forum gathered over 180 participants from Ukraine, Poland, and Slovakia who are committed to addressing global and regional environmental challenges through cross-border cooperation and the development of eco-skills for sustainable development. The event served as a platform for scientists, educators, and environmental professionals from three European countries to share experiences in effective medical waste management, monitoring biological invasions, utilizing green energy to combat climate change, exploring biofortification prospects, and assessing the environmental impact of agricultural activities. A special emphasis was placed on the importance of various forms of cooperation among project participants to foster ecological skills and environmental awareness. Members of the NGO "EcoInitiative" Participate in the First Event of the International Grant Project "Enhancing Eco-Skills Through Cross-Border Cooperation Between V4 and Ukraine No. 22420189"
On November 20, 2024, the first event of the International Grant Project "Enhancing Eco-Skills Through Cross-Border Cooperation Between V4 and Ukraine No. 22420189" took place — an international online webinar titled "Collaborative Strategies for Regional Eco-Challenges."

The event brought together 186 participants from Ukraine, Poland, and Slovakia into a community focused on addressing global and regional ecological challenges in their countries through sustainable development initiatives and cross-border cooperation. Environmental experts from three European countries presented their latest research on the impacts of environmental pollution and climate change on wildlife, the ecological condition of rivers, ecosystem transformation processes caused by non-native species, innovative ecological developments utilizing microorganisms for environmental cleaning and restoration, waste bioconversion for the green transformation of European countries, and achieving climate neutrality in the long term. Webinar participants actively engaged in discussions with experts, shared their experiences, and exchanged knowledge on successful environmental research initiatives and methodologies.
